Real scenario

You have subscriptions spread across personal tools, business tools, phone apps, and free trials. The lesson shows how to turn that mess into a small agent workflow that remembers renewal dates and checks in before money leaves your account.

Prompt you can reuse

“Create a subscription tracker for me. Add Claude Pro at $20/month on the 12th, Canva Pro at $13/month with a trial ending June 20, Google One at $9.99/month on the 3rd, and ChatGPT Plus at $20/month on the 18th. Remind me 3 days before each renewal and give me a monthly keep, cancel, or downgrade review.”

Agent builds

I created the tracker, saved each renewal date, flagged Canva as trial risk, calculated your visible monthly spend at $62.99, and set a recurring review command so we can check value before each charge.

What the lesson teaches
1. Enter messy subscription details in plain English.
2. Turn them into a clean tracker with price, renewal date, category, and owner.
3. Save reminder rules and a monthly review command.
4. Ask the agent to identify trial risk, duplicate tools, and subscriptions you forgot about.

A virtual computer is an agent that can work across digital surfaces for you. Browser tabs, files, notes, search, docs, and workflows become part of one operating system instead of isolated tools.

Does it actually remember me?

That is the point. The system is built around durable memory, searchable context, saved facts, and reusable skills so you stop re-explaining the same preferences and projects.
